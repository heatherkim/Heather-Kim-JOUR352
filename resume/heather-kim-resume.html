About Me

Currently I am a junior Broadcast Journalism major and Asian American Studies minor at the University of Maryland, College Park. As a photographer, I always keep two goals in mind. The first, to make those around me feel comfortable and secure, always valuing people before the shot. The second, to constantly observe the hearts and hands of those that I encounter. Whether it's reporting on marginalized communitites or taking high school senior pictures, these goals pertain to all of my work - no exceptions.

Contact Me

Passionate

I'm driven by those who came before me and hope to leave a path for those that come after. The world of photojournalism needs more diverse voices and I'm here to keep the conversation going.

Visual Storyteller

Through my studies, travels and interactions, I've learned that above all else, people come before the shot. My goal as a photographer is to continue to observe the hearts and hands of those that I encounter and continue to tell their stories accurately, fairly and wholeheartedly.

Invested

Whether it's high school portraits, breaking news or fashion, I'm invested in telling the story. To quote late activist Yuri Kochiyama, "we are all part of one another."

Photo Editor

I photograph for the University of Maryland’s only on-campus fashion magazine. My responsibilities include collaborating with creatives in the DMV area. This usually entails traveling off campus, conducting a 1-2 hour photoshoot and editing hundreds of photos in time before seasonal publication. Besides shooting cover stories, I also pitch and execute original content for the magazine.
Videographer

I created highlight videos that documented the experiences of over 500 campers, all of whom came from inner-cities around the east coast. I recorded, edited and produced these videos by myself and shared these videos weekly across social media platforms to direct more traffic to the camp’s site and garner more attention for the camp in general.
